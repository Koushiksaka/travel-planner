DAY - 1 SURAT

Surat has a long history dating back to ancient times and has been an important center for trade and commerce since the Mughal era. It was a major port on the Arabian Sea and was known as the "Gateway to Mecca" for pilgrims traveling from the Indian subcontinent. DAY - 2 VADODARA

Vadodara, also known as Baroda, is a city located in the state of Gujarat, India. It is the third-largest city in Gujarat and serves as the administrative headquarters of Vadodara district. With a population of over 2 million people, Vadodara is a thriving cultural, industrial, and educational hub. The city is known for its grand palaces, temples, museums, and gardens. The magnificent Lakshmi Vilas Palace, the residence of the Gaekwad dynasty, stands as a symbol of architectural brilliance. Vadodara has a well-developed infrastructure, with an international airport, a railway junction, and an extensive network of roads.

DAY - 3 AHMEDABAD

Ahmedabad is the largest city in the state of Gujarat, India. It is an important cultural, commercial, and industrial hub with a rich history dating back to ancient times. The city is situated on the banks of the Sabarmati River. Ahmedabad is well-connected with an international airport, a railway network, and a comprehensive public transportation system. It offers a mix of traditional and modern attractions, making it a fascinating destination for both tourists and business travelers.

DAY - 4 BHAVNAGAR AND DIU

Part - 1 : Nishkalank Mahadev Temple is a Hindu temple located at Koliyak near Bhavnagar, Gujarat. Situated in Koliyak Beach, it is one of the rarest sea temples in India you must include in your Gujarat trip and among the most visited places of pilgrimage in Bhavnagar. Located about a kilometer into the sea, the temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple has 5 distinct swayambhu Shiva lingams on a square platform and each is having a Nandi statue facing it. This temple is submerged during high tides in the sea and emerges during low tides to reveal itself majestically, promising its devotees to wash away all sins. During the high tide, the idol of the lord is submerged and all that can be seen are the flag and a pillar. The temple was built with special care to withstand high tides and it truly remains an unsolved mystery to modern engineers and technology experts. The temple is believed to be built by the Pandavas after the Kurukshetra War. Legend says that after killing all the Kauravas, Pandavas began to feel guilty for their sins. To discover reclamation for their sins, Pandavas consulted Krishna who handed them a black flag and a black cow and asked them to trail and said when both the flag and the cow turn white, they will all be forgiven. Lord Krishna also asked them to apologize to Lord Shiva after that. They followed the cow everywhere it took them and acknowledged the flag for many years to various places, yet the shades did not change. Finally, when they reached the Koliyak Beach, both turned white and soon the Pandavas meditated on Lord Shiva and apologized for the sins committed by them. Lord Shiva impressed by their prayers, showed up in lingam form to each brother. Hence, they named it as Nishkalank Mahadev which means being spotless, clean, and innocent.

Part - 2 : Diu, officially known as the Union Territory of Daman and Diu, is a picturesque coastal town located on the southern tip of the Saurashtra peninsula, near the state of Gujarat, India. The town is known for its beautiful and serene beaches, such as Nagoa Beach, Ghoghla Beach, and Jallandhar Beach. These sandy stretches offer a tranquil atmosphere and breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea. Water sports like swimming, parasailing, and jet skiing are popular activities for visitors.

DAY - 5 SOMNATH

Somnath is a sacred city located in the Gir Somnath district of Gujarat, India. It is famous for the historic Somnath Temple, which is one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Hindus. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and holds great religious significance. The city offers other attractions for visitors. The Somnath Beach, located near the temple, provides a serene and picturesque setting for relaxation and enjoyment. Somnath is not only a place of religious significance but also a destination that offers a glimpse into Gujarat's glorious past and cultural heritage

DAY - 6 DWARAKA

Dwarka, located in the westernmost part of Gujarat, India, is an ancient and sacred city with great religious significance. It is believed to be the dwelling place of Lord Krishna, an important deity in Hindu mythology. Dwarka is situated on the Arabian Sea coast and is one of India's Char Dham pilgrimage sites. The main attraction in Dwarka is the Dwarkadhish Temple, dedicated to Lord Krishna. The temple, built in the Chalukya style of architecture, is a significant pilgrimage site for devotees of Lord Krishna. It is believed to be situated at the same place where Lord Krishna's original Dwarka city stood.

DAY - 7 RANN OF KUTCH

The Rann of Kutch is a vast salt marsh located in the Thar Desert of the Kutch district in the state of Gujarat, India. It is one of the largest salt deserts in the world, covering an area of about 7,505 square kilometers (2,897 square miles). The Rann of Kutch is known for its unique and mesmerizing landscape, with vast stretches of white salt-encrusted plains that extend as far as the eye can see.During the monsoon season, the Rann of Kutch gets submerged in water, and it becomes a marshland. However, in the dry season, the water evaporates, leaving behind a layer of salt crystals that give the region its distinctive appearance. The Rann of Kutch is characterized by its arid climate, extreme temperatures, and sparse vegetation.
